Overview

Atherosclerotic narrowing of coronary arteries leading to myocardial ischemia. Includes stable angina, acute coronary syndromes, and silent ischemia. This comprehensive resource provides evidence-based information on clinical presentation, diagnosis, treatment, and management of Coronary Artery Disease.

Key Facts

  • • 6.2% of adults (18.2 million) affected population
  • • 365,000 deaths annually annual impact
  • • Very Common prevalence in clinical practice
  • • High clinical severity classification

This condition falls under the category of Ischemic Heart Disease and requires comprehensive evaluation and management. Early recognition and appropriate treatment are crucial for optimal outcomes.

Major

Diabetes Mellitus

Increases CAD risk by 2-3 fold in men and 3-7 fold in women. Considered a CAD equivalent.

Moderate

Physical Inactivity

Sedentary lifestyle increases CAD risk by 50%. Regular exercise reduces risk by 25-30%.

Moderate

Obesity

BMI >30 kg/m² increases risk, particularly central obesity with waist circumference >40 inches (men) or >35 inches (women).

Non-Modifiable Risk Factors

Major

Age

Risk increases significantly with age. Men >45 years, women >55 years (or post-menopausal).

Major

Sex

Men develop CAD earlier than women. Women's risk increases significantly after menopause.

Major

Family History

First-degree relative with premature CAD (men <55 years, women <65 years) increases risk by 2-3 fold.

Moderate

Race/Ethnicity

Higher prevalence in South Asian, African American, and Hispanic populations compared to Caucasians.

Moderate

Genetic Factors

Specific genetic polymorphisms affecting lipid metabolism, inflammation, and thrombosis.

Diagnosis

Initial Evaluation

Clinical Assessment

  • • Detailed symptom history
  • • Risk factor assessment
  • • Physical examination
  • • 12-lead ECG
  • • Chest X-ray
  • • Basic laboratory tests

Laboratory Tests

  • • Cardiac troponins (if ACS suspected)
  • • Complete blood count
  • • Comprehensive metabolic panel
  • • Lipid profile
  • • HbA1c (if diabetic risk)
  • • Thyroid function tests

Diagnostic Tests

First-Line

Exercise Stress Testing

Indications
  • • Intermediate pre-test probability CAD
  • • Evaluation of known CAD
  • • Functional capacity assessment
  • • Risk stratification
Interpretation
  • • ST depression ≥1mm = positive
  • • Exercise duration <5 METS = high risk
  • • Hypotensive response = poor prognosis
  • • Angina during test = high risk
Second-Line

Imaging Studies

Stress Echo

Combines exercise/pharmacologic stress with echocardiography. Wall motion abnormalities indicate ischemia.

Nuclear Stress

Myocardial perfusion imaging with SPECT or PET. Perfusion defects indicate ischemia or scar.

Cardiac CT

Coronary CT angiography for anatomical assessment. Calcium scoring for risk stratification.

Invasive Testing

Coronary Angiography

Indications
  • • High-risk stress test results
  • • Unstable angina
  • • STEMI or NSTEMI
  • • Survivors of sudden cardiac death
  • • Pre-operative assessment for high-risk surgery
Significant Stenosis
  • • ≥70% diameter stenosis = significant
  • • ≥50% left main stenosis = significant
  • • FFR ≤0.80 = functionally significant
  • • iFR ≤0.89 = functionally significant

Treatment

Lifestyle Modifications

Dietary Changes

  • • Mediterranean or DASH diet
  • • Limit saturated fat to <7% of calories
  • • Limit trans fat to <1% of calories
  • • Increase fruits, vegetables, whole grains
  • • Limit sodium to <2,300mg daily
  • • Moderate alcohol consumption only

Exercise Program

  • • 150 minutes moderate aerobic activity weekly
  • • 75 minutes vigorous activity weekly
  • • Resistance training 2 days per week
  • • Gradual progression in intensity
  • • Cardiac rehabilitation post-MI
  • • Stress testing before vigorous exercise

Medical Therapy

Essential

Antiplatelet Therapy

Aspirin
  • • Dose: 81mg daily (preferred)
  • • Alternative: 325mg daily if needed
  • • Lifelong therapy for all CAD patients
  • • Reduces MI risk by 25-30%
P2Y12 Inhibitors
  • • Clopidogrel 75mg daily
  • • Ticagrelor 90mg BID
  • • Prasugrel 10mg daily
  • • Dual therapy post-ACS or stent
Essential

Statins (HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors)

High-Intensity Statins
  • • Atorvastatin 40-80mg daily
  • • Rosuvastatin 20-40mg daily
  • • Reduce LDL-C by ≥50%
  • • Target LDL-C <70 mg/dL in very high risk
Benefits
  • • 25-35% LDL-C reduction
  • • 20-30% reduction in CV events
  • • Plaque stabilization
  • • Anti-inflammatory effects
Class I

Beta-Blockers

Preferred Agents
  • • Metoprolol succinate 25-200mg daily
  • • Carvedilol 3.125-25mg BID
  • • Bisoprolol 2.5-10mg daily
  • • Titrate to resting HR 55-60 bpm
Indications
  • • Post-MI patients
  • • Stable angina
  • • Heart failure with reduced EF
  • • Hypertension

Revascularization Strategies

Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI)

  • • Primary PCI for STEMI within 90 minutes
  • • Elective PCI for high-risk anatomy
  • • Drug-eluting stents preferred
  • • Dual antiplatelet therapy 6-12 months
  • • FFR-guided PCI for intermediate lesions

Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG)

  • • Left main disease (>50% stenosis)
  • • Three-vessel disease with diabetes
  • • Two-vessel disease with proximal LAD
  • • SYNTAX score >22
  • • Failed PCI or unsuitable anatomy
